# Hypothesis Quality Criteria

## Testability
A good hypothesis can be tested with specific observations or experiments.

## Falsifiability
A good hypothesis can be shown wrong by clear contrary evidence.

## Parsimony
Prefer the simplest explanation that still fits the evidence.

## Explanatory Power
A good hypothesis explains more than a single isolated observation.

## Scope
State the boundaries of the hypothesis clearly.

## Consistency
The hypothesis should align with well-supported knowledge unless there is strong justification.

## Novelty
Useful hypotheses should add insight, not just restate known facts.

## Comparing Multiple Hypotheses
When several explanations are possible, focus on which ones make distinct predictions and are easiest to test.
